Lesson Briefing

Read before starting class. Do not show this screen to the student.

A1–A2Main SeriesDaily RoutinesSimple Present~50 min

Objective: The student will be able to describe daily routines using simple present — including the He/She + verb+S rule — and apply vocabulary naturally in speaking, using the video as a contextual anchor.

Vocabulary (5 words)

  • wake up
  • hit snooze
  • make breakfast
  • head out
  • commute

Grammar target

  • I + base verb
  • She/He + verb+S
  • doesn't + base verb
  • Does she…?

Wolf Method reminders

  • Student speaks 80%
  • No yes/no questions
  • Always use speaking starters
  • Student infers grammar first

A1 Accessibility tips

  • Use starters on every screen
  • Allow L1 if needed
  • Pause video at key moments
  • Praise attempts, not perfection

Pre-teach pronunciation: commute /kəˈmjuːt/ and routine /ruːˈtiːn/. Encourage the student to speak aloud — not just read. If the student is shy, always begin with the speaking starters on screen.

Transcript

Key Moments from the Video

These are the most important parts. Tap the yellow words to understand the grammar.

⏱ 0:05
"I normally start my morning around 8am and I wake up to the sound of my alarm clock. I'm not a morning person, so sometimes I will hit snooze."
wake up — With I, we use the BASE form: I wake up.
With She / He, we add -S: She wakes up.
⭐ This is the Simple Present rule!
hit snooze — With I: I hit snooze.  |   With She: She hits snooze.
Notice: hit → hits ← She always gets the -S!
⏱ 1:20
"After I wake up, I get out of bed and stretch. Then I head straight to the bathroom to wash my face and brush my teeth."
get out of bed → She gets out of bed (she = +S)
These three verbs together form a phrasal verb: get + out + of bed.
head straight to = go directly to (without stopping).
With I: I head straight… | With She: She heads straight…
⏱ 2:40
"Now it's time to make breakfast. I normally don't like to eat a typical American breakfast. I like eating fruits and veggies."
make breakfast — in English we say make breakfast, NOT ~~cook breakfast~~.
She: She makes breakfast.   I: I make breakfast.
don't like — Negative with I: I don't like…
Negative with She: She doesn't like… ← no -S on the verb after doesn't!
⏱ 3:55
"After eating, I make the bed. I like starting the day with a clean room. Then I head out the door and commute to work. It takes me about 15 minutes."
make the bed = arrange the sheets and pillows to look neat.
She: She makes the bed.
head out = leave, go outside. Informal and very natural.
"She heads out at 8am." — "I head out at 8am."
commute = travel regularly between home and work.
As a verb: She commutes by subway.
As a noun: "My commute takes 20 minutes."

Grammar

Simple Present — The Rule

You already discovered the pattern in the transcript. Now let's make it clear.

SubjectFormExample
Ibase verbI wake up at 8.
You / We / Theybase verbThey commute by bus.
He / She / It ⭐verb + SShe wakes up at 8.

⭐ Only He / She / It gets the -S. Always.

Negatives: don't vs doesn't

SubjectNegativeExample
I / You / We / Theydon't + verbI don't eat meat.
He / She / Itdoesn't + verbShe doesn't eat meat.

⭐ After doesn't, the verb stays BASE form — no extra S!

Spelling: verb + S rules

Verb ends in…RuleResult
Most verbs+ swakes, makes, likes
-ch / -sh / -o / -x+ eswatches, goes
consonant + yy → iesstudies, carries
haveirregularhas

Conversation Practice

A Real Conversation

Read this conversation. Then answer the questions and practise speaking it with your teacher.

🧑
Hey! What time do you usually wake up?
👩
I wake up at 8am. But I always hit snooze first! 😅
🧑
Ha! Me too. Do you eat breakfast before work?
👩
Yes! I make breakfast every morning. I don't like fast food. I prefer fruits and eggs.
🧑
That sounds healthy! How do you get to work?
👩
I commute by subway. It takes about 15 minutes. I like it — I listen to music!
🧑
Nice! What time do you head out?
👩
Around 8:45. Before I leave, I always make the bed. I need a clean room!
